Persuasion & Psychological Manipulation Cues

Urgency & Temporal Pressure
Fraud calls frequently induce time pressure ("act now," "within 24 hours") to suppress deliberation.
How to extract: 
- Urgency lexicons + regex ("immediately", "expires")
- Imperative language detection through Part-of-Speech tagging
- "Do not hang up." "Press 1 now." "Transfer the money today."
- Transformer-based urgency scoring (fine-tuned SBERT/BERT)

Authority Invocation & Institutional Impersonation
Scammers impersonate banks, government agencies, or executives using institutional register.
How to extract: 
- Named Entity Recognition for orgs/agencies ("IRS", "Bank of America")
- Authority-title patterns ("Agent Smith", "Fraud Department")
- Embedding similarity to legitimate institutional communication templates

Fear & Threat Language 
Threat language ("arrest," "account suspended") is a dominant compliance driver.
How to extract: 
- Threat lexicons + negative emotion categories
- Conditional threat parsing ("If you don't X, then Y")
- Emotion or threat classifiers 

Trust-Building & Manipulation
Fraudsters establish familiarity and reciprocity to lower suspicion.
How to extract: 
- Personalization detection (recipient name, role entities)
- Style matching against known legitimate sender profiles

Conversational Dynamics (throughout) 

Information Elicitation Patterns - Verification of Personal Info 
Fraud is strongly characterized by credential solicitation framed as "verification."
How to extract: 
- Regex for personal info such as SSN, DOB, account numbers, passwords 
- Question classification (information-seeking speech acts)
- Verb-frame triggers ("confirm", "verify", "submit")

Request Escalation & Compliance Framing
Calls often follow staged escalation: small verification → sensitive demand → payment.
How to extract: 
- Dialogue act tagging (directive/request density)
- Sequential Transformer models  
- Sensitivity tracking of requests across conversation timeline

Coherence and Consistency 

Narrative Inconsistency & Contradiction Signals
Fabricated narratives degrade under questioning, producing inconsistencies.
How to extract: 
- Natural Language inference (NLI) contradiction scoring across turns
- Check referential consistency
- Temporal consistency checking ("yesterday" vs "last month")

Lexical & Syntactic Anomalies (Declining Utility)
Traditional stylometric cues (grammar errors, odd phrasing) worked for legacy scams but are brittle against LLM-generated fraud.
How to extract: 
- LLM detection of odd phrasing/ wrong grammar
- May be increasingly weak with AI usage.
